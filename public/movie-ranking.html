The Best Movies According To Mahesh Babu

My Top Movies Of All Time

SALAAR

Critics and audiences have generally praised the film's high-octane action sequences, Prabhas's commanding screen presence, and the chemistry between Prabhas and Prithviraj. The world-building of Khansaar is also noted as a strong point, with its intricate details and lore.

JERSY

The story is told through the perspective of a grown-up son named Nani, who is reading a book about his father, Arjun (played by Nani). The film flashes back to 1996, where Arjun is a talented but failed cricketer in his mid-30s. He gave up the sport a decade earlier and is now unemployed, struggling to make ends meet for his wife, Sarah (Shraddha Srinath), and his son.

JAANU

The story follows K. Ramachandra, or Ram (Sharwanand), a successful travel photographer. While on a trip, he finds himself overwhelmed by nostalgia as he passes by his old high school. This leads to a reunion being organized with his old classmates from the 2004 batch. At the reunion, Ram finally meets his childhood sweetheart, S. Janaki Devi, or Jaanu (Samantha), who now lives in Singapore and is married with a child.
